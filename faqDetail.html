A story of diving into the front-end development and redesign of a FAQ

—Overview— The start of it all


Jumbo Privacy is an app startup that aims to help users manage their online presence and protect their data privacy. The scope of this project was ambiguous because my only given goal was just to make the overall user experience of the FAQ better. The somewhat nebulous guidelines made it difficult for me to initially decide where to start. However, observation of the user experience from two different perspectives: from interactions through tickets and from user interviews helped inform what design decision paths I needed to pursue.



—Process— The journey into the jungle that is the user


First, I conducted 5 usability test sessions in order to identify user pain points. I found that the main areas for improvement were the navigational structure of articles and hard-to-follow tutorials. For the former particularly, the pain point was due to each individual article existing on its own discrete page, making it tedious for users to go back and forth if they wanted to read multiple articles in one session.


Then, over the course of 20 journey mapping interviews, I discovered that many of our users followed a similar journey in getting to Jumbo. Another interesting point was that almost all of the users would open Jumbo in short bursts, and some while on the go.


Finally, Jumbo's Support team mainly communicates with users over email and often provides links to resources for them. To make it easier for Support to direct users to helpful articles, I knew that I wanted my future redesign to continue supporting this particular support interaction.



—Design decisions— The changes born from my research


After conducting my research, one of the first changes I made was to organize articles into a one-page layout that a user can scroll through using one hand, making it easy to navigate Jumbo even on the go. With one tap, each article can be expanded or collapsed for easy viewing.


Tutorials now have an associated lightbox. With just a tap, users are able to enlarge the images and follow the tutorial text at the same time. They can also jump to any point in the tutorial using the image previews at the bottom of the lightbox.


Incorporating the one-page layout also meant that I had to change how links worked. In the new FAQ, each article is assigned its own unique ID. By adjusting the ID in the URL, the Support team can directly send a user to any article on a certain page, and, through some JavaScript, automatically expand the question to reduce friction for the user as much as possible.
