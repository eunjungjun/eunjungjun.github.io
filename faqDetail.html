Jumbo Privacy User FAQ

A story of diving into the front-end development and redesign of a FAQ

—Overview— The start of it all


Jumbo Privacy is an app startup that aims to help users manage their online presence and protect their data privacy. The scope of this project was ambiguous because my only given goal was just to make the overall user experience of the FAQ better. The somewhat nebulous guidelines made it difficult for me to initially decide where to start. However, observation of the user experience from two different perspectives: from interactions through tickets and from user interviews helped inform what design decision paths I needed to pursue.

—Process— The journey into the jungle that is the user


Pain points were tedious navigation and tutorials


First, I conducted 5 usability tests to identify pain points. In particular, the navigation suffered from each individual article having its own page, making it tedious for users to go back and forth to read multiple articles.


People used Jumbo in short bursts and on the go


Then, through 20 journey mapping interviews, I discovered many of our users followed similar journeys in getting to Jumbo. Another interesting point was that almost all users would open Jumbo in short bursts, and some while on the go.


Redesign should bolster Support-User interactions


Finally, the Support team communicates with users over email and often provides links to resources for them. I knew that I wanted my future redesign to make it easier for Support to direct users to helpful articles.



—Design decisions— The changes born from my research


One page-one hand navigation


After conducting my research, the first change I made was to organize articles into a one-page layout that could be scrolled with one hand, making it easy to navigate Jumbo even on the go. With a tap, each article can be expanded or collapsed.


Lightbox-based tutorials


Tutorials now have an associated lightbox. With just a tap, users are able to enlarge the images and follow the tutorial text at the same time. They can also jump to any point in the tutorial using the image previews at the bottom of the lightbox.


Easily linking users to specific articles they need


Incorporating the new layout meant I had to rework links. Each article was assigned its own unique ID--by adjusting the ID in the URL, Support can send users to any article, and the JavaScript automatically expands it to reduce friction.
