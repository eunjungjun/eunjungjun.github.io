Jumbo Privacy User FAQ

A story of diving into the front-end development and redesign of a FAQ

Overview

Jumbo Privacy is an app startup that aims to help users manage their online presence and protect their data privacy. The scope of this project was ambiguous: make the overall user experience of the FAQ better in order to expand the Support team resources. The somewhat nebulous guidelines made it difficult for me to initially decide where to start. However, I was working in a user support and success role and was able to observe the user experience from two different perspectives: from interactions through tickets and from user interviews, and these experiences helped inform what design decision paths I needed to pursue.


Process

Through 5 usability test sessions, I found the main areas for improvement were the navigational structure of articles and hard-to-follow tutorials.


Over the course of 20 journey mapping interviews, I discovered that almost all of the users would open Jumbo in short bursts, and some while on the go.


Jumbo's Support team mainly communicates with users over email. To make it easier for Support to direct users to helpful articles, any provided FAQ links should go directly to the article in question.

Design decisions

Multiple articles are now organized into a one-page layout that a user can scroll through using one hand, making it easy to navigate Jumbo on the go. With one tap, each article can be expanded or collapsed for easy viewing.


Tutorials now have an associated lightbox that allows users to enlarge the images and follow the tutorial text at the same time.


Each article in the one-page layout is assigned its own unique ID. By adjusting the ID in the URL, this minimizes the user's cognitive load and reduces the number of messages the Support team sends to answer user questions.
